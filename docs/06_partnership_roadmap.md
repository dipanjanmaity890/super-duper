# 06 — Partnership Roadmap

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–6)

### Premier League (PLDTS) — CRITICAL PATH
Without PL sign-off: no official club branding, no Tracab data, no VAR access, no PLDTS event stream.

**Google's leverage:**
- YouTube already pays the PL for content rights
- Google Cloud is a credible infrastructure partner
- The PL wants tech innovation — pitch as "official second screen of the Premier League"
- Unlocks all 20 clubs simultaneously (vs negotiating club-by-club)

**Negotiation timeline:** 6–12 months  
**Pitch angle:** Distribution partner for growing global PL fanbases in Asia and North America

### Stats Perform (Opta) — Independent Deal
Commercial data license for F9 event feed + speed data. Already has relationships with most broadcast partners.

**Cost:** ~£2–4M/year for full PL live feed at scale  
**Timeline:** 2–3 month procurement  
**Note:** Can be signed in parallel with PL talks — no dependency

---

## Phase 2 — Enrichment (Months 4–9)

### Sky Sports / TNT Sports — Broadcast Sync
Sky and TNT hold the PL broadcast rights. For ACR sync to work cleanly, need their broadcast timecode via a partner API.

**Status:** Preferred, not required (ACR fingerprinting is the fallback)  
**Value to Sky:** Google promotes YouTube TV as the premium viewing surface

### StatsBomb — xG Enrichment Layer
Adds xG, xA, and 360° pressure data on top of Opta's event stream. StatsBomb has existing PL club partnerships — a Google deal gives StatsBomb brand visibility for a consumer product.

**Timeline:** 1–2 months  
**Cost:** ~£400/match

---

## Phase 3 — Scale (Months 9–18)

### Club Direct Deals — Top 6 First
Individual club data (STATSports GPS, training insights, squad depth signals) requires per-club agreements.

**Priority order:** Arsenal, Man City, Liverpool, Chelsea, Spurs, Man Utd  
**Why these 6:** ~65% of global PL fan engagement  
**Google's offer:** Global distribution for fan growth in Asia + North America

### Champions League / La Liga Expansion
Same stack extends to UCL (UEFA data partnership) and La Liga.

**Marginal cost:** Low — Opta and StatsBomb already cover both competitions  
**Accelerant:** UEFA is Google Cloud's existing partner in stadium tech
